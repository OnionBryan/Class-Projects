Variance Corrections

  • Sampling Error: Adjusted for random fluctuations due to sample sizes
  • Measurement Error: Corrected using reliability coefficients
  • Range Restriction: Not corrected (assumed minimal in leadership studies)
  • Sample-Weighted Means: Larger studies given proportionally more weight
Critical Assumption: Independence of artifacts - potentially violated by nested data (multiple criteria per study), which could inflate confidence intervals by ~10-15%.

Statistical Intervals Explained

80% Credibility Interval (CV)

Estimates variability of individual correlations across studies

Interpretation: If CV excludes zero, >90% of correlations are positive

Example: Transformational CV = .10 to .78 (includes low values, suggesting context matters)

90% Confidence Interval (CI)

Estimates sampling error around mean correlation

Interpretation: If CI excludes zero, mean effect is statistically significant

Example: Transformational CI = .39 to .49 (tight, reliable estimate)
